The mechanism

Three things every grappler over 35
should know about his own tendons.

01

Tendons are cables. Yours are fraying.

Every tendon in your body is a tightly braided cable of thousands of collagen fibers locked together in tension. Each hard roll causes microscopic damage. At 25, your body rebuilt the cable overnight. At 45, the damage outpaces the repair year after year. Fibers separate. The cable thins. That’s what you feel as “tendinitis.” It isn’t inflammation. It’s structural.

02

One cell rebuilds them. It’s gone quiet.

Inside every tendon there is one cell type responsible for laying down new collagen and cross-linking it back into the cable. The fibroblast. Active fibroblasts mean tendons remodel. Dormant fibroblasts mean tendons degrade. Past 35, blood flow drops, age-related signals fall, and the cell goes silent. The cable stops getting rebuilt. This is the part most sports medicine doesn’t address.

Moringa Oleifera contains a portfolio of phytochemicals — quercetin, kaempferol, isothiocyanates, beta-sitosterol — that signal the fibroblast directly to start building.
